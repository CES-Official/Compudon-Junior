COMPUDON JUNIOR Official Rules

BINDING AGREEMENT

PARTICIPATION IN THIS CONTEST CONSTITUTES YOUR ACCEPTANCE OF THESE OFFICIAL RULES.

In order to participate in the COMPUDON Junior Championship (the “Championship”), you must agree to these Rules. Therefore, please read these Rules prior to participating to ensure you understand and agree. You agree that participating in the Championship constitute your agreement to these Rules. You may not participate in the Championship and are not eligible to receive any prize described in these Rules unless you agree to these Rules. These Rules form a binding legal agreement between you and Organizer with respect to the Championship.


CHAMPIONSHIP ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible to participate in the Championship, you must: (1) have taken a qualifying COMPUDON Junior (Leve1) exam between the set date range (Please refer Important Dates section on home page for exact dates); (2) be at least 8 years or older as on August 15, 2025; (3) be 14 or under 14 years as on August 15, 2025; (4) you must have the permission from your parent(s) or legal guardian(s) to participate in the Championship and (5) must be enrolled in an approved academic institution.


CHAMPIONSHIP PERIOD

Please check the home page for the Important Dates of the championship.


HOW TO PARTICIPATE IN THE CHAMPIONSHIP

It is preferred that students participate in the championship through their schools. In special cases, where School is not participating but an otherwise eligible student(s) wants to participate then they can do so individually. However, their participation is subject to written confirmation by the Organizer.

There will be three rounds in the championship

  • 1st Round –students will take a qualifying exam within the School.
  • 2nd Round will be India/National Finals Round –Top 300 qualifying students from each category will be invited to India Finals.
  • 3rd Round will be International Round- and the Top 3 winning positions in each participating/ qualifying country will compete with top 3 winners of other countries. In case the number of participants from any country is not sufficient to participate in the international round then that country will not be allowed to participate. And if the particip0ating countries are less than 3, then the 3rd round will not be held and only Country Level Winners will be announced.

Entrants will be required to test in English language only. No other language is available. During the Qualifying Round/ Level 1, eligible entrants will take the eligible COMPUDON Junior exam based on objectives of Season VI. The objectives are given on the home page under the Objectives tab; however, Organizer reserves the right to alter the curricula/objectives for this championship with prior information.


QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

The qualification criteria to go to India Finals of the championship is highest score in the Level 1/ Qualifying Round. In the event of a tie, the entrant that completed the applicable exam in the shortest amount of time (as determined by Organizer, in its sole discretion) will be shortlisted as the qualifying candidate for the India Finals.

Candidates must complete the Qualifying Exam/ Level 1 exam within the pre-defined time limit. The time limit for students aged 8 to 10 is 30 minutes and for students above 10 years of age is 45 minutes. After the expiry of the assigned time, the system will automatically submit the exam. Exams are void if they are in whole or part incomplete, altered, counterfeit, obtained through fraud, or late.


WINNER DETERMINATION

At the conclusion of the Championship Period, the one (1) exam that receives the highest score will be deemed a potential First Prize winner/Gold Medalist/ Country Champion.

The one (1) exam that receives the next highest score in the exam will be deemed a potential Second Prize Winner/ National 1st Runner Up / National Silver Medalist. The one (1) exam that receives the next highest score in the exam will be deemed a potential Third Prize Winner / National 2nd Runner Up/ National Bronze Medalist. Similarly, there will be Regional Winners in the qualifying regions.

In the event of a tie for any or all of the above positions, the entrant that completed the applicable exam in the shortest amount of time (as determined by the Organizer, in its sole discretion) will be identified as the potential winner. In the event a potential winner is disqualified for any reason, the candidate with the next highest total score and least time taken will be chosen as the potential winner.

WINNER NOTIFICATION: On the day & date given on home page, the winners will be announced. Winners or respective teachers in charge of the winner’s school will be notified by phone/SMS at the number provided during final exams.

Except where prohibited by law, each potential winner / Parents/ Legal Guardian may be required to sign and return a Declaration of Eligibility and provide any additional information that may be required / requested by Organizer.

All notification requirements, as well as other requirements within these Rules, will be strictly enforced. In the event that no exams are received in any region, no prize will be awarded. Determinations of Organizer are final and binding.
